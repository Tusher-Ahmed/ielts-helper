🏗️ ৪-প্যারা কাঠামো — প্রতিটা প্যারা কীভাবে লিখবে

1️⃣ Introduction (২ বাক্য)

বাক্য ১ — Paraphrase: প্রশ্নটা নিজের ভাষায় লেখো।
বাক্য ২ — Thesis: তোমার অবস্থান + এসে কী বলবে তার ইঙ্গিত।

"It is often argued that [topic paraphrased]. This essay will argue that [your position], as [reason 1] and [reason 2]."

2️⃣ + 3️⃣ Body Paragraphs — TEE ফর্মুলা

T — Topic sentence (প্যারার মূল আইডিয়া)
E — Explain (কেন? বিস্তারিত ব্যাখ্যা)
E — Example (বাস্তব/বিশ্বাসযোগ্য উদাহরণ)

To begin with, [topic sentence]. This is because [explanation]. For instance, [specific example]. As a result, [mini-conclusion linking back]."

4️⃣ Conclusion (১–২ বাক্য)

"In conclusion" + মতামত restate + কোনো নতুন আইডিয়া না।

"In conclusion, while [acknowledge other side], I firmly believe that [restate position] because [summary of reasons]."

💡 আইডিয়া বের করার কৌশল (blank মাথা এড়াও)

  • Ask "why?" & "example?": প্রতিটা দাবির পেছনে কারণ ও একটা উদাহরণ খোঁজো।
  • Categories: ভাবো — economic, social, health, environmental, educational দিক থেকে।
  • Stakeholders: কারা প্রভাবিত হয়? — individuals, government, businesses, students।
  • উদাহরণ বানানো যায়: "A recent study suggests…" / "In many countries…" — নির্দিষ্ট হলেই ভালো।

🏗️ সর্বজনীন কাঠামো

  1. Introduction (১ বাক্য): প্রশ্নটা paraphrase করো — chart কী দেখাচ্ছে।
  2. Overview (২ বাক্য): সবচেয়ে গুরুত্বপূর্ণ ২–৩টি ট্রেন্ড/বৈশিষ্ট্য (সংখ্যা ছাড়া)। এটাই সবচেয়ে বেশি নম্বর টানে।

✅ Overview-তে "Overall," দিয়ে শুরু করো — examiner সরাসরি এটা খোঁজে।

প্রতিটা chart type কীভাবে ধরবে

📈 Line graph (সময়ের সাথে পরিবর্তন)

Trend ভাষা ব্যবহার করো: rose, fell, peaked, fluctuated। সময় গুরুত্বপূর্ণ।

Overview: Overall, [X] rose steadily over the period, whereas [Y] showed a downward trend, with both converging by the end.

📊 Bar chart (তুলনা)

Comparison ভাষা: higher than, the highest, twice as many as, compared with।

Overview: Overall, [category] had by far the highest figure, while [category] accounted for the smallest proportion.

🥧 Pie chart (অনুপাত)

Proportion ভাষা: accounts for, makes up, a quarter, the majority, a fraction।

Detail: [X] made up nearly half of the total, whereas [Y] accounted for just under a fifth.

📋 Table (ডেটা)

সব ডেটা লিখো না — সবচেয়ে বড়, ছোট ও লক্ষণীয় বেছে নাও। গ্রুপ করে তুলনা করো।

⚙️ Process diagram

Overview = কতগুলো ধাপ + শুরু-শেষ কোথায়। Passive + sequence শব্দ: First, Next, After that, Finally, is then + V3।

🗺️ Map (পরিবর্তন)

Location ও change ভাষা: to the north of, was replaced by, was demolished, a new X was built। Past passive যদি অতীত হয়।

The forest to the east was cleared and replaced by a residential area.

Task 2 — Opinion (Agree/Disagree)

প্রশ্ন: Some people believe that children should be taught how to manage money at school. To what extent do you agree or disagree?

BAND 8 MODEL

It is increasingly argued that financial literacy should form part of the school curriculum. I completely agree with this view, as teaching children to manage money equips them with an essential life skill and helps to prevent serious financial difficulties later in life.

To begin with, money management is a genuinely practical skill that people rely on throughout their entire lives. Unlike many purely academic subjects, budgeting, saving, and understanding interest are applied on an almost daily basis. For instance, a child who learns to set aside a portion of their pocket money is far more likely to develop responsible spending habits as an adult, and to appreciate the value of saving for the future. Consequently, schools that neglect this area risk leaving students poorly prepared for the realities of independent adult life.

Furthermore, early financial education can significantly reduce the risk of debt. A large number of young adults fall into financial trouble simply because they were never taught how credit cards, loans, and interest actually work. A clear example of this is the growing number of university students who take on substantial loans they barely understand, and who consequently struggle for years afterwards. Had these individuals received proper guidance at school, many such problems could have been avoided altogether, benefiting both them and society as a whole.

In conclusion, I strongly believe that schools should teach money management, since it is a vital life skill that fosters personal responsibility and protects young people from future financial hardship. Governments would therefore be wise to make financial literacy a standard part of every child's education.

Task 2 — Discussion (Both views)

প্রশ্ন: Some people prefer to work for a large company, while others prefer a small one. Discuss both views and give your opinion.

BAND 8 MODEL

Choosing between working for a large corporation and a small firm is a decision that many job-seekers face at some point in their careers. While both options have clear merits, I personally believe that smaller companies offer greater opportunities for long-term professional growth.

On the one hand, large companies provide a number of notable advantages, chiefly job security and clearly structured career paths. Employees often benefit from higher salaries, formal training programmes, and generous benefits such as pensions and healthcare. For example, large multinational firms typically offer well-defined promotion ladders, which strongly appeals to those who value stability and predictability in their working lives. Such security can be particularly reassuring for individuals who have families or long-term financial commitments to support.

On the other hand, smaller companies allow individuals to take on a much wider range of responsibilities and to see the direct impact of their work on the business. As a software engineer, I have personally found that, within a smaller team, one acquires new skills far more quickly, simply because there is no one else to rely on. This kind of hands-on, varied experience is, in my view, invaluable, particularly in the early stages of a career, as it builds both confidence and versatility.

In conclusion, although large firms undeniably offer greater security and structure, I believe that the diverse experience and rapid learning gained in a small company ultimately contribute more to an individual's long-term professional development. For ambitious people, that experience often outweighs the comfort of stability.

Task 2 — Problem / Solution

প্রশ্ন: Traffic congestion is a serious problem in many cities. What are the causes, and what measures can solve it?

BAND 8 MODEL

Traffic congestion has become a pressing issue in urban areas across the world, causing frustration, wasted time, and increased pollution. This essay will examine the main causes of the problem and propose some realistic and effective solutions.

The primary cause of congestion is the sheer rise in private car ownership. As incomes increase, more and more people are able to buy their own vehicles, placing enormous strain on road networks that were never designed to handle such volumes of traffic. In addition, inadequate and unreliable public transport forces many commuters to depend on their cars, since they see no convenient alternative, which only worsens the situation during peak hours.

To tackle this problem, governments should invest heavily in efficient and affordable public transport, such as underground and rapid bus systems, making it a genuine alternative to driving. A further effective measure is congestion charging, which requires drivers to pay a fee to enter busy city centres. This approach has proven successful in cities such as London, where it has noticeably reduced both traffic and air pollution by discouraging unnecessary car journeys. In addition, encouraging cycling and remote working, wherever possible, can further ease the pressure on busy roads during peak hours.

In conclusion, traffic congestion stems largely from rising private car use and weak public transport, but it is far from unsolvable. With sustained investment in modern transport systems and the introduction of smart policies such as congestion charging, the problem can be substantially reduced, creating cleaner and more liveable cities for everyone.

💻 Technology — Opinion (Agree/Disagree)

প্রশ্ন: Some people believe that smartphones and other modern devices are making people less able to communicate with each other in person. To what extent do you agree or disagree?

BAND 8 MODEL

There is a growing concern that modern devices, particularly smartphones, are eroding people's ability to communicate face to face. While I acknowledge that these devices can be distracting, I largely disagree with the view that they have made us unable to interact in person.

It is true that smartphones sometimes interfere with real-life communication. In many social settings, people can be seen staring at their screens rather than talking to those around them, which can weaken the quality of face-to-face conversations. Younger people, who have grown up with this technology, may in particular become overly reliant on texting and struggle with more direct forms of interaction. In this sense, there is some truth to the argument.

However, I would argue that smartphones have, on the whole, improved communication rather than damaged it. These devices allow people to stay in constant contact with friends and family who live far away, strengthening relationships that distance might otherwise weaken. Moreover, the skills required for face-to-face communication are still developed through everyday interactions at school, at work, and within families. The problem, therefore, lies not with the devices themselves but with how responsibly they are used. Indeed, many people rely on their phones precisely in order to arrange to meet friends and colleagues in person.

In conclusion, although smartphones can certainly distract people during in-person conversations, I disagree that they have made us incapable of communicating face to face. With sensible habits, such as putting devices away during meals and meetings, technology can enhance our relationships rather than harm them.

📱 Social Media — Two-part question

প্রশ্ন: Social media platforms are used by millions of people every day. Why have they become so popular, and is this a positive or a negative development?

BAND 8 MODEL

Social media has become an inseparable part of modern life, used daily by millions across the globe. This essay will first explain the reasons behind its remarkable popularity and then argue that, on balance, it is a positive development.

There are several reasons why social media has become so popular. Firstly, it allows people to stay connected with friends and family instantly and free of charge, no matter how far apart they are. Secondly, it offers endless entertainment and a constant flow of news, all accessible from a single device that most people carry everywhere. In addition, it gives ordinary individuals a platform to express their opinions and share their creativity, an opportunity that was unavailable to most people in the past. These features make it both highly convenient and deeply engaging.

In my opinion, despite some genuine concerns, this development is more positive than negative. On the negative side, excessive use has been linked to anxiety, and the rapid spread of false information is a serious issue. However, the benefits are considerable: social media helps people maintain valuable relationships, raises awareness of important social causes, and provides small businesses with an affordable way to reach new customers. Used responsibly, these advantages clearly outweigh the drawbacks.

In conclusion, social media owes its popularity to the connection, entertainment, and freedom of expression it provides. Although it undoubtedly carries certain risks, I firmly believe that, when used with care, it is ultimately a positive force in society.

🎓 Education — Discussion (Both views)

প্রশ্ন: Some people believe that online learning is just as effective as attending a traditional classroom, while others disagree. Discuss both views and give your own opinion.

BAND 8 MODEL

In recent years, online learning has expanded rapidly, leading many to question whether it can truly match the traditional classroom. While both approaches have clear strengths, I believe that a combination of the two is the most effective.

On the one hand, there are strong arguments in favour of online learning. It offers unmatched flexibility, allowing students to study at their own pace, at a time and place that suits them. This is particularly valuable for working adults or those with family commitments. Furthermore, online courses often provide access to expert teachers and materials from around the world. For motivated, self-disciplined learners, online study can be highly effective.

On the other hand, supporters of traditional classrooms argue that face-to-face teaching offers benefits that are difficult to replicate online. In a physical classroom, students can ask questions immediately, engage in lively discussion, and benefit from the direct support of a teacher who can sense when they are struggling. Classrooms also provide valuable social interaction, helping young people to develop communication skills and form friendships. For younger learners in particular, this daily contact with peers and teachers is a vital part of growing up.

In conclusion, while online learning provides remarkable flexibility and access, I believe it cannot completely replace the personal support of a traditional classroom. In my view, the ideal approach is a 'blended' model that combines the convenience of online study with the human connection of face-to-face teaching, thereby drawing on the strengths of both.

🏥 Health — Opinion (Agree/Disagree)

প্রশ্ন: Some people believe that individuals are responsible for their own health, while others think the government should play a greater role. To what extent do you agree that the government should take responsibility for people's health?

BAND 8 MODEL

The question of who is responsible for people's health has become increasingly important as lifestyle-related illnesses rise. While personal choice clearly plays a role, I largely agree that governments have a significant responsibility to protect public health.

There are strong reasons to support government involvement. Firstly, only governments have the power to shape the environment in which people make choices. By taxing unhealthy products such as sugary drinks, funding public healthcare, and running awareness campaigns, they can make healthy living easier and more affordable for everyone. Secondly, poor public health places an enormous financial burden on society, since treating preventable diseases is extremely costly. For these reasons, it is both in the government's interest and its duty to promote healthier lifestyles.

Nevertheless, it would be wrong to ignore the importance of personal responsibility. Ultimately, individuals decide what to eat, whether to exercise, and how to live, and no government can force people to make healthy choices. If citizens are unwilling to take care of themselves, even the best policies will have limited effect. Therefore, personal effort and government action must work together. The most successful outcomes are likely to come from a genuine partnership between the state and its citizens.

In conclusion, although individuals must take responsibility for their own habits, I agree that governments have a vital role to play in protecting public health. By creating an environment that encourages healthy choices, governments can support their citizens, while individuals must still do their part to lead healthier lives.

🌍 Environment — Opinion (Agree/Disagree)

প্রশ্ন: Some people believe that environmental problems are too large for individuals to solve, and that only governments and large companies can make a difference. To what extent do you agree or disagree?

BAND 8 MODEL

It is sometimes argued that environmental issues are so vast that only governments and corporations can address them, leaving individuals powerless. While I agree that large organisations bear the greatest responsibility, I disagree that individual action is meaningless.

On the one hand, it is undeniable that governments and major companies have the greatest capacity to bring about change. Only governments can pass laws to limit pollution, invest in renewable energy, and protect natural habitats on a national scale. Similarly, large corporations, which are responsible for a significant proportion of global emissions, can transform their practices in ways no single person could. For this reason, meaningful progress does depend heavily on those in positions of power.

On the other hand, I firmly believe that individuals also have an important role to play. When millions of people reduce waste, save energy, and choose environmentally friendly products, the combined effect is substantial. Moreover, individuals influence change indirectly, by pressuring companies through their purchasing choices and by voting for governments with strong environmental policies. In this way, collective individual action can shape the very decisions made by those larger bodies. History shows that widespread public pressure has often forced both governments and companies to adopt greener policies.

In conclusion, while governments and companies undoubtedly hold the primary responsibility for tackling environmental problems, I disagree that individuals are helpless. Real progress requires action at every level, with the choices of ordinary people both making a direct difference and encouraging those in power to act.

💼 Work — Advantages / Disadvantages

প্রশ্ন: In many countries, a growing number of people are choosing to work from home rather than in a traditional office. Do the advantages of this trend outweigh the disadvantages?

BAND 8 MODEL

In recent years, working from home has become increasingly common, a trend accelerated by advances in technology. While this shift brings certain drawbacks, I would argue that its advantages clearly outweigh them for most workers.

The most significant benefit of working from home is the flexibility it offers. Without the need to commute, employees save a considerable amount of time and money, which can instead be devoted to family, rest, or personal interests. This often leads to a healthier work-life balance and, in many cases, higher productivity, as workers can structure their day around the hours when they are most focused. Furthermore, employers benefit too, since they can reduce expensive office costs and recruit talented staff regardless of their location.

There are, admittedly, some disadvantages. Working from home can blur the boundary between professional and personal life, sometimes resulting in longer hours and, in extreme cases, burnout. Employees may also feel isolated, missing the social interaction and spontaneous collaboration of an office. However, these problems can largely be overcome through good habits and modern tools such as video conferencing, which allow teams to stay connected. Many companies have also adopted hybrid arrangements in order to enjoy the best of both worlds.

In conclusion, although working from home presents genuine challenges relating to isolation and work-life boundaries, the substantial gains in flexibility, time, and cost make its advantages far more significant. In my view, provided it is managed sensibly, remote working is a positive development that is likely to remain a permanent feature of modern employment.

⚖️ Crime — Discussion (Both views)

প্রশ্ন: Some people believe that the best way to reduce crime is to give longer prison sentences, while others think there are more effective alternatives. Discuss both views and give your own opinion.

BAND 8 MODEL

How best to reduce crime is a question that has long divided opinion. While some people advocate longer prison sentences as the most effective deterrent, others believe that alternative approaches are more successful. This essay will consider both views before giving my own.

On the one hand, supporters of tougher sentencing argue that the threat of a long prison term discourages potential criminals. Keeping offenders locked away for longer also protects the public by preventing them from committing further crimes. From this perspective, strict punishment demonstrates that society takes crime seriously and provides justice for victims. In cases of serious or violent crime, such an approach may well be justified.

On the other hand, many experts argue that prison alone does little to address the root causes of crime, such as poverty, lack of education, and drug addiction. Long sentences can even make matters worse, as prisons sometimes serve as 'schools for crime', where reoffending becomes more likely. Alternatives such as education, job training, and rehabilitation programmes aim to tackle these underlying problems, helping offenders reintegrate into society. Such measures are often far cheaper than long-term imprisonment and tend to produce more lasting results.

In conclusion, while longer prison sentences may be appropriate for the most serious offences, I believe that, for the majority of crimes, prevention and rehabilitation are far more effective. By addressing the causes of criminal behaviour rather than simply punishing it, society is more likely to achieve a lasting reduction in crime.

🌐 Globalisation & Culture — Discussion

প্রশ্ন: Some people believe that globalisation is a threat to local cultures, while others think it helps cultures to develop. Discuss both views and give your own opinion.

BAND 8 MODEL

Globalisation has connected the world as never before, and its effect on local cultures is a subject of considerable debate. While some fear that it threatens cultural identity, others believe it enriches cultures. This essay will examine both views before giving my opinion.

On the one hand, there are legitimate concerns that globalisation endangers local cultures. As international brands, films, and fashions spread across the world, distinctive local traditions, languages, and customs can be pushed aside. Younger generations, in particular, may come to prefer a globalised, largely Western lifestyle over their own heritage. In this way, the world risks becoming culturally uniform, with unique traditions gradually disappearing.

On the other hand, many argue that globalisation actually helps cultures to grow and thrive. Increased contact between nations allows people to share ideas, food, music, and art, exposing them to new influences that can enrich their own culture. Moreover, globalisation gives smaller cultures a global audience; traditional crafts and cuisines can now be appreciated by people all over the world. From this viewpoint, cultural exchange leads to development rather than destruction. In many cases, contact with other cultures also increases people's pride in their own traditions.

In conclusion, although globalisation certainly poses some risks to local traditions, I personally believe that its overall effect is positive. Rather than destroying cultures, it encourages them to interact and evolve. The key, in my view, is to embrace the benefits of cultural exchange while making a conscious effort to preserve what makes each local culture unique.
