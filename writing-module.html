1️⃣ Introduction (২ বাক্য)

বাক্য ১ — Paraphrase: প্রশ্নটা নিজের ভাষায় লেখো।
বাক্য ২ — Thesis: তোমার অবস্থান + এসে কী বলবে তার ইঙ্গিত।

"It is often argued that [topic paraphrased]. This essay will argue that [your position], as [reason 1] and [reason 2]."

2️⃣ + 3️⃣ Body Paragraphs — TEE ফর্মুলা

T — Topic sentence (প্যারার মূল আইডিয়া)
E — Explain (কেন? বিস্তারিত ব্যাখ্যা)
E — Example (বাস্তব/বিশ্বাসযোগ্য উদাহরণ)

To begin with, [topic sentence]. This is because [explanation]. For instance, [specific example]. As a result, [mini-conclusion linking back]."

4️⃣ Conclusion (১–২ বাক্য)

"In conclusion" + মতামত restate + কোনো নতুন আইডিয়া না।

"In conclusion, while [acknowledge other side], I firmly believe that [restate position] because [summary of reasons]."

💡 আইডিয়া বের করার কৌশল (blank মাথা এড়াও)

  • Ask "why?" & "example?": প্রতিটা দাবির পেছনে কারণ ও একটা উদাহরণ খোঁজো।
  • Categories: ভাবো — economic, social, health, environmental, educational দিক থেকে।
  • Stakeholders: কারা প্রভাবিত হয়? — individuals, government, businesses, students।
  • উদাহরণ বানানো যায়: "A recent study suggests…" / "In many countries…" — নির্দিষ্ট হলেই ভালো।

Task 2 — Opinion (Agree/Disagree)

প্রশ্ন: Some people believe that children should be taught how to manage money at school. To what extent do you agree or disagree?

BAND 8 MODEL

It is increasingly argued that financial literacy should form part of the school curriculum. I completely agree with this view, as teaching children to manage money equips them with an essential life skill and helps to prevent serious financial difficulties later in life.

To begin with, money management is a genuinely practical skill that people rely on throughout their entire lives. Unlike many purely academic subjects, budgeting, saving, and understanding interest are applied on an almost daily basis. For instance, a child who learns to set aside a portion of their pocket money is far more likely to develop responsible spending habits as an adult, and to appreciate the value of saving for the future. Consequently, schools that neglect this area risk leaving students poorly prepared for the realities of independent adult life.

Furthermore, early financial education can significantly reduce the risk of debt. A large number of young adults fall into financial trouble simply because they were never taught how credit cards, loans, and interest actually work. A clear example of this is the growing number of university students who take on substantial loans they barely understand, and who consequently struggle for years afterwards. Had these individuals received proper guidance at school, many such problems could have been avoided altogether, benefiting both them and society as a whole.

In conclusion, I strongly believe that schools should teach money management, since it is a vital life skill that fosters personal responsibility and protects young people from future financial hardship. Governments would therefore be wise to make financial literacy a standard part of every child's education.

Task 2 — Discussion (Both views)

প্রশ্ন: Some people prefer to work for a large company, while others prefer a small one. Discuss both views and give your opinion.

BAND 8 MODEL

Choosing between working for a large corporation and a small firm is a decision that many job-seekers face at some point in their careers. While both options have clear merits, I personally believe that smaller companies offer greater opportunities for long-term professional growth.

On the one hand, large companies provide a number of notable advantages, chiefly job security and clearly structured career paths. Employees often benefit from higher salaries, formal training programmes, and generous benefits such as pensions and healthcare. For example, large multinational firms typically offer well-defined promotion ladders, which strongly appeals to those who value stability and predictability in their working lives. Such security can be particularly reassuring for individuals who have families or long-term financial commitments to support.

On the other hand, smaller companies allow individuals to take on a much wider range of responsibilities and to see the direct impact of their work on the business. As a software engineer, I have personally found that, within a smaller team, one acquires new skills far more quickly, simply because there is no one else to rely on. This kind of hands-on, varied experience is, in my view, invaluable, particularly in the early stages of a career, as it builds both confidence and versatility.

In conclusion, although large firms undeniably offer greater security and structure, I believe that the diverse experience and rapid learning gained in a small company ultimately contribute more to an individual's long-term professional development. For ambitious people, that experience often outweighs the comfort of stability.

The case for the corporate giant rests largely on stability and structure. Security is comforting, but the versatility, ownership and steep learning curve that a compact organisation demands lay a far sturdier foundation for the decades that follow. Task 2 — Problem / Solution

প্রশ্ন: Traffic congestion is a serious problem in many cities. What are the causes, and what measures can solve it?

BAND 8 MODEL

Traffic congestion has become a pressing issue in urban areas across the world, causing frustration, wasted time, and increased pollution. This essay will examine the main causes of the problem and propose some realistic and effective solutions.

The primary cause of congestion is the sheer rise in private car ownership. As incomes increase, more and more people are able to buy their own vehicles, placing enormous strain on road networks that were never designed to handle such volumes of traffic. In addition, inadequate and unreliable public transport forces many commuters to depend on their cars, since they see no convenient alternative, which only worsens the situation during peak hours.

To tackle this problem, governments should invest heavily in efficient and affordable public transport, such as underground and rapid bus systems, making it a genuine alternative to driving. A further effective measure is congestion charging, which requires drivers to pay a fee to enter busy city centres. This approach has proven successful in cities such as London, where it has noticeably reduced both traffic and air pollution by discouraging unnecessary car journeys. In addition, encouraging cycling and remote working, wherever possible, can further ease the pressure on busy roads during peak hours.

In conclusion, traffic congestion stems largely from rising private car use and weak public transport, but it is far from unsolvable. With sustained investment in modern transport systems and the introduction of smart policies such as congestion charging, the problem can be substantially reduced, creating cleaner and more liveable cities for everyone.

✅ শব্দ সংখ্যা: ≈ 257 (Task 2 এর ২৫০+ পূরণ)
🏆 BAND 9 MODEL

Gridlocked streets have become an almost daily ordeal for city dwellers, and while the phenomenon may seem intractable, its origins are reasonably clear and its remedies well within reach. The congestion that paralyses modern cities springs chiefly from unchecked car dependency, yet a blend of better alternatives and demand management can loosen the grip considerably.

At the heart of the problem lies the relentless growth in private vehicle ownership. As disposable incomes climb, a car ceases to be a luxury and becomes a default, flooding road networks that were laid out for a fraction of today's volumes. Compounding this is the chronic weakness of public transport in many places; where buses are infrequent and trains overcrowded, commuters quite rationally retreat to the privacy and reliability of their own cars, and rush hour duly grinds to a halt. Poor urban planning, which scatters homes far from workplaces, only entrenches these journeys.

Reversing the trend therefore hinges on making driving the less attractive option rather than the obvious one.
